about me

My passion for exploring design and technology began when I was very young, about 4 or 5 years old, and in a somewhat unusual way.


After school, I'd play games on my friend's playstation that allowed me to design in within them. For example, I loved racing games that in the garage I could put custom colors and graphics onto my cars. Similarly with skating games - designing on the bottom of the skateboard, and a bmx game that would allow me to design a skate park. Eventually, my parents bought me and my brother the Xbox original console. I became infatuated with these games, not so much for the racing, or the challenges, but the amazing ways you could design your cars and boards. I'd spend hours and hours on a car, getting creative as I could with the design. I made "wraps" for cars before wraps were a common thing. I loved getting reactions from my friends and hearing what they thought about my "work".


What these games did a really good job at was showing how to design in a carefree environment. Granted, they don't have all the capabilites of modern day design tools for UX and UI such as Sketch and Illustrator. In a way, however, this limitation helped breed creativity, forcing me to create with the design elements that were available and stretching out elements to the Nth degree to get the results I wanted.


I knew throughout my life I wanted to do something with design.


After high school I went to school in Boston taking a web development program at General Assembly. It was a wonderful experience and a very demanding, intense program. Before and after the program I continually learn new things on my own, simply because I am passionate about this field.


Since, I have worked with startups, small businesses, and nonprofits that are nimble and innovative, blending thoughtful design with viable development.


I have brought a number of complex ideas and product features to market, leading design within cross-functional teams to bring the creative idea to reality. While working in design, having a background in the development side allows me an understanding of the limitations of systems and appreciate the amount of work that must go into a given feature. I'm technically proficient in skills such as storyboarding to high fidelity mockups to interpreting how analytics data measures up in our product's design. I enjoy wearing lots of different hats as needed. 


My design philosophy includes in part from the book The Tao of Design and User Experience by Andrew Ou. It is that the best experience is no experience. What that means to me is this: when you - the user - don't have any friction, you can easily accomplish that what you came there to do, and will then have a positive experience with the company. 


I have a knack for creating apps and pages that have users delighted with, analysing relevant data and metrics to continually improve them.
